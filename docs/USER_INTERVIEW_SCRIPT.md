# User Interview Script

Keep each conversation to 20 minutes. Do not pitch the product until after understanding the current process.

## Introduction

“I’m researching how small businesses decide whether a public-sector opportunity is worth pursuing. I’m not selling anything. I want to understand the real workflow and where people lose time.”

## Questions

1. Tell me about the last public tender you considered.
2. How did you first find it?
3. Who read the documents and made the decision?
4. What did you check before deciding to apply?
5. Which part took the longest?
6. Have you ever discovered a disqualifying requirement late in the process?
7. What information is hardest to interpret?
8. What documents do you repeatedly have to locate or recreate?
9. How long does the initial go/no-go decision normally take?
10. What would you need to trust an automated analysis?
11. Would you share one real tender for a free test?
12. Who else should I speak with?

## Evidence to capture

- original tender PDF;
- current checklist or spreadsheet;
- time spent on initial review;
- reason for applying or declining;
- missed or confusing requirements;
- exact words the user uses to describe the problem.

## Closing test

Show the report only after the interview. Ask:

- “What is the first thing you would check here?”
- “What do you distrust?”
- “Which section would change your decision?”
- “Would you use this on another tender next week?”
